# Morning Sunlight Protocol

**Source:** Huberman Lab - sleep / circadian episodes.

**Why:** Early morning sunlight (within 30-60 min of waking) hits melanopsin-containing ipRGCs in the lower retina and signals the suprachiasmatic nucleus. This triggers the cortisol awakening response, suppresses melatonin, and starts a 12-14 hour countdown to evening melatonin secretion.

**Protocol:**
- Step outside within 30-60 minutes of waking.
- 5-10 minutes on a bright day; 15-20 minutes on a cloudy day; 30+ minutes if overcast.
- No sunglasses; regular prescription glasses fine.
- Do NOT look at the sun. Diffuse outdoor brightness is enough.

**Why it matters for the Captain:** Boosts daytime alertness, sharpens reaction time 7-10%, and most importantly, anchors sleep onset that night.
